WELLHEAD PRODUCTION ACTIVITY

Daily · NASA VIIRS satellite
GULF PRODUCTION INDEX — % of pre-crisis baseline

Gas flaring at oil wellheads is a direct byproduct of crude production. When output is curtailed, flaring drops. This section tracks daily thermal satellite detections (NASA VIIRS) as a proxy for production activity — comparing today's level to the pre-crisis baseline.

The VIIRS sensor aboard NOAA-20 and Suomi-NPP satellites detects thermal anomalies globally on every orbit, publishing data daily through NASA FIRMS with 3–12 hour latency. Fire Radiative Power (FRP) measures flare intensity in megawatts — not just a binary detection, but a quantitative signal. Summing FRP across oil field bounding boxes gives a production activity index used by energy research firms as a leading indicator of upstream supply.

Caveats: cloud cover can suppress detections; flaring may vary due to maintenance or gas capture policy changes independent of production levels. The 7-day rolling average smooths satellite pass variability. This is a directional proxy, not a precise barrel count.
